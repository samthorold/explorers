# The reproductive-nutrient earmark is funded by all nutrient income, not only autotrophic uptake

The `(1 − kappa)` reproductive-nutrient earmark is fed by **every** unit of nutrient an agent acquires, split at the moment of acquisition regardless of route: autotrophic uptake from the available pool (flow 2) **and** the nutrient ingested by consuming a body or carcass (flow 3). The kappa allocation is a property of the organism's life history, not of how the nutrient was acquired — exactly as on the energy side, where the kappa split in the grow phase (flow 9) partitions surplus reserve whatever flow filled it (photosynthesis or consumption). This extends [ADR-0003](0003-nutrient-embodiment-and-reproductive-earmark.md), whose principle ("nutrient takes the same shape as energy… fed the `(1 − kappa)` share of each tick's uptake") was right but was wired to a single acquisition route.

## Problem

ADR-0003 gave nutrient a reproductive earmark mirroring `repro_reserve`, fed the `(1 − kappa)` share of "each tick's uptake." In both the spec and the implementation, "uptake" meant **only flow 2** — extraction from the available pool — and pool uptake is gated on the autotrophy trait (the sessile root/hypha infrastructure). The nutrient a consumer gains from flow 3 is deposited entirely in the free store; it never passes through the kappa split. So a pure heterotroph hits **two** independent locks on reproductive nutrient:

- **Pool uptake** is the autotroph's route — a `photosynthetic_absorption = 0` agent draws nothing, so the one code path that feeds the earmark never runs for it.
- **Consumption** does reach its body, but routes 100% to the free store (growth), 0% to the earmark.

Result: an agent that "converts other agents' bodies into energy and nutrients" can *grow* on prey but can **never** accumulate a reproductive-nutrient earmark, so it can never clear `reproduction_nutrient_threshold`. **Heterotrophy is somatic-only: predation sustains a body but never a lineage.** No parameter setting changes this — like the autotroph lockout ADR-0003 itself fixed, it is structural.

This silently contradicts the rest of the system design. The energy-flow narrative states nutrient income enters "through uptake from the available pool (flow 2) **or alongside energy during consumption (flow 3)**"; the trophic-viability sections require consumers and decomposers to be reproducing trophic levels ("A consumer whose stoichiometric demand exceeds what prey provides is nutrient-limited despite successful feeding"; "decomposers… acquire nutrient from carcasses… closing the nutrient cycle"). It also contradicts the **energy** side, which already credits predation toward reproduction: consumption energy lands in reserve and the grow-phase kappa split routes its `(1 − kappa)` share into `repro_reserve`, source-agnostically. Energy was symmetric across acquisition routes; nutrient was not.

The ecology is unambiguous (`docs/ecology/nutrient-cycling.md`, *Stoichiometric constraints*). Heterotrophs acquire their limiting nutrient **by ingesting tissue**, not from the mineral pool — herbivores process carbon-rich food to extract nitrogen; predators eat stoichiometrically similar tissue. Under homeostasis an organism builds its entire body from that ingested budget, and reproductive tissue (eggs, offspring) is body — it carries body stoichiometry and is funded from the same acquired nutrient. An animal makes offspring out of the nitrogen and phosphorus it ate. A model in which ingested nutrient can enlarge the soma but can never become offspring has no ecological counterpart.

## Considered options

- **Leave the earmark fed by pool uptake only (rejected).** Faithful to neither the ecology nor the rest of the spec; bars an entire trophic level from reproducing and makes the decomposition chain a non-starter, defeating the nutrient-cycling the model exists to show.
- **Split nutrient at acquisition, both routes (chosen).** Apply the same `(1 − kappa)` earmark split to consumption nutrient (flow 3) that flow 2 already applies. The split becomes route-agnostic, mirroring energy. The consumer retains nutrient up to its **total** homeostatic demand — somatic *and* reproductive — and splits what it retains by kappa; excess beyond demand is still excreted to the pool (consumer-driven recycling preserved). Each trophic strategy funds reproduction from its own nutrient route: autotrophs from pool uptake, heterotrophs from ingested biomass.
- **Split at a single free-store sink instead of at each source (considered).** The truest mirror of energy (whose split happens at the grow-phase sink) would be a per-tick split of free-store inflow wherever it lands, so any future nutrient source is covered automatically. Rejected for now only because nutrient has no retention/"surplus above buffer" concept to hang a sink-side step on — which is *why* ADR-0003 split at the source — and the source-side fix is the smaller, local change. Worth revisiting if more nutrient sources are added.

## Consequences

- **Heterotroph lineages can reproduce.** A consumer or decomposer that feeds well accumulates a reproductive-nutrient earmark and provisions offspring from ingested nutrient, exactly as a producer provisions from absorbed nutrient. The grazer chain and the detrital chain both become demographically closed.
- **Energy and nutrient are symmetric across acquisition routes.** Both currencies are split by the same kappa whatever flow delivered them. The "one allocation gene, both currencies" property of ADR-0003 now holds for every trophic strategy, not just autotrophs.
- **The intended autotroph property is untouched.** "Mobile photosynthesis is a demographic dead end" still holds — it follows from a *mobile autotroph* being unable to run sessile pool uptake, not from any blanket rule, and heterotrophs were never the subject of that claim. The producer/consumer divide remains an emergent consequence of cost structure.
- **Consumer-driven nutrient recycling is preserved and sharpened.** Retention is still capped at the consumer's stoichiometric demand and the non-limiting excess excreted to the pool (Sterner & Elser 2002; the herbivore that excretes the nutrient it has in surplus). The only change is that "demand" now spans soma + reproduction and the retained fraction is allocated by kappa.
- **Reproductive nutrient inherits prey stoichiometry.** A consumer can only earmark what its prey carried; a predator eating biochemically similar tissue retains more than a herbivore grazing nutrient-poor structure (trophic-distance efficiency, flow 7). Nutrient scarcity propagates up the food chain and throttles consumer fecundity without any special rule.
- **Conservation unchanged.** The split only relabels where retained nutrient sits inside the agent; total agent nutrient, excretion to the pool, and parent→offspring transfer are all as before.
- **Implementation touches the two consumption nutrient-retention sites** (living-target drain and carcass drain) and the definition of consumer nutrient demand (to include the reproductive share); the autotrophic uptake path is already correct. Supersedes the "fed by flow 2 only" reading of ADR-0003.
