# Viability

The *a priori* lens on the world rules. [World rules](world-rules.md) say what the mechanisms are; [expected properties](expected-properties.md) say what should emerge if the mechanisms are calibrated well. This document sits between them and asks: **given a parameterization, can it produce the expected properties at all — before we ever run the simulation?**

Formally, the [execution model](execution-model.md) defines a discrete dynamical system: the tick loop is a map `state_{t+1} = T(state_t)` whose phase order is fixed. Viability analysis is the study of that map's fixed points — **where in world-parameter space does `T` admit a non-trivial, persistent living state, and where does every trajectory collapse into one of the degenerate configurations?** This is not implementation; it is the mathematics of the map the execution model specifies, and it is the most detailed element of system design that remains implementation-agnostic.

## What this document is, and is not

This document **commits nothing**. It introduces no new mechanism, no new functional form, no new parameter. Every result here is a *consequence* of what the world rules and execution model already commit. Its sole output is the **functional form of the parameter regions that cannot work** — the regions where no amount of search will find life, because the physics forbids it.

This has a direct payoff for [world genesis](world-rules.md). The genesis search is expensive (ensembles of replicate runs); viability analysis is nearly free (arithmetic). Running the gates first **shrinks the search box** by excluding provably-dead parameterizations before any ensemble is spent. The search never goes away — that is its purpose — but it should not waste runs on a region viability can rule out in closed form.

When a region's deadness *cannot* be decided here, that is itself a finding: the system design is under-committed at that point. The fix is to commit the missing detail in system design (world rules / execution model) — never to invent it here.

## How much can be decided a priori

The sharpness of a gate is bounded by how much of the map `T` is pinned. Three tiers:

- **Fully pinned** — the phase order; the kappa flow-allocation rule; the linear nutrient demand `demand = base_nutrient_ratio + spec_coeff · (autotrophy + heterotrophy + mobility)`; the route-agnostic nutrient split; both conservation laws; proportional competition; the reproduction gate. Here the math is exact, symbolic in a few constants.
- **Form pinned, coefficients searched** — convex/superlinear trait maintenance; movement cost linear in distance; the monotone structural death threshold; the exponential efficiency-vs-distance trophic-transfer form (`base_trophic_efficiency · exp(−trophic_distance_decay · d)`, [world rules](world-rules.md), flow 7). Here the math is parametric: real inequalities and comparative statics.
- **Open** — the photosynthesis income magnitude; the wear accumulation/repair law. Here only structural or bounding statements are possible.

A gate is cheap and decidable today exactly when it rests on the first two tiers.

## Organising principle: the failure modes as gates

The degenerate configurations in [expected properties](expected-properties.md) are the natural index. Each is a way the map `T` fails to hold a living fixed point, and they split by how amenable they are to a priori analysis:

- **Existence failures — cheaply provable dead-zones.** These ask "does a living fixed point exist at all?" — exactly what static analysis answers. *Extinction* and *energy death* are gated below.
- **Dynamics failures — out of cheap a priori reach (for now).** *Monoculture* vs. coexistence, *frozen dynamics* vs. oscillation, *population explosion*, *generalist dominance*, and *nutrient lockup* are not about whether a fixed point exists but about its *stability and multiplicity* — bifurcation and stability territory. (Nutrient lockup shares the nutrient-floor lineage of the energy-death gate but is realized dynamically, through decomposer reach and turnover rather than a static `N_total` inequality; it is the one mode of this group now *partially* reached — a conditional decomposer-return floor and a closed-form flux-balance characterisation, both below.) (Generalist dominance was once filed as a candidate existence gate; the analysis below shows its prevention rests on structural fragility and functional incompatibility — a mortality-and-morphology force, not a static income/cost inequality — so it belongs here.) The rest remain the domain of the search until the affirmative theory is built out; nutrient lockup is where its first piece has been placed. (Two of these — *frozen↔oscillation* and *monoculture↔coexistence* — now carry a closed-form *distance-to-bifurcation* reading wired into the search as a **descriptor plus cross-check**, not a gate; see [genesis search, *Predicted bifurcation coordinates and the cross-check*](genesis-search.md#predicted-bifurcation-coordinates-and-the-cross-check). These readings are conductance-aware: by reserve conservation the mobilised growth flow equals net income at any reserve fixed point, so the branching margin and the fixed-point location are invariant to the [reserve mobilisation rate](world-rules.md) `f`, while the oscillation reading carries reserve as an explicit slow state so its Hopf boundary tracks `f`.)

> **Direction of travel.** Today this document is a set of *negative gates* — proofs that a parameterization does **not** fail in a given way. In future these can be reoriented toward the *affirmative* — constructive conditions under which a property **will** appear. The gates are the negative-space sketch of that eventual theory. The first step of that reorientation is taken below for **nutrient lockup**: a conditional decomposer-return floor and a closed-form flux-balance characterisation (`C* = death flux ÷ decomposition rate`) now stand alongside its negative classification — affirmative where the committed fluxes permit, and honestly short of a cheap gate where decomposer mass is emergent.

## Gate — extinction (flux floor)

*Form-free.* Producer income is bounded by the solar flux in its light-competition neighbourhood; an isolated producer receives at most the full flux magnitude `F`. Metabolism charges at least the base rate `B` every tick (flow 8). So the per-tick net of any producer is at most `F − B`.

> **If `F ≤ B`, no producer is ever net-positive — no energy enters the living system — extinction, for every other parameter and every functional form.**

This is the weakest necessary condition for life — clearing it is far from sufficient — but failing it is a guaranteed kill, decidable from two numbers.

## Gate — energy death (nutrient floor)

*Fully pinned.* Nutrient is conserved. A living agent holds free nutrient, a reproductive earmark, and nutrient bound in structure (`structure · demand`). For a lineage to persist, at least one agent must be simultaneously **embodied** (`structure > 0`, required even to photosynthesise — light share is weighted by structure, flow 1) and **reproduction-ready** (earmark `≥ N_repro_threshold`, the reproduction nutrient gate). Both draw on the conserved total. Hence a necessary condition:

```
N_total  ≥  structure_min · (base_nutrient_ratio + spec_coeff · σ_min)   +   N_repro_threshold
              └──── nutrient bound in one minimal viable body ────┘            └─ earmark to clear the gate ─┘
```

where `σ_min` is the specification sum of a minimal viable agent.

> **If `N_total` is below this, no agent can be both embodied and able to reproduce — no reproduction, no persistence — regardless of solar flux. Energy abundance cannot rescue a nutrient-starved world.**

The gate is deliberately conservative: it ignores nutrient locked in carcasses in transit (death flux ÷ decomposition rate — given closed form by the flux-balance characterisation below) and the available pool that ongoing uptake needs, both of which only *raise* the real floor. So failing the closed form is sufficient for deadness; clearing it is necessary, not sufficient. This is the analytic statement of the [energy death](expected-properties.md#energy-death) failure mode and the [no-passive-decay](world-rules.md#no-passive-decay) consequence.

The carcass-locked nutrient this static gate deliberately sets aside is exactly what the [nutrient lockup](expected-properties.md#nutrient-lockup) failure mode tracks dynamically: a world can clear `N_total` here yet still strand its nutrient in an unconsumed dead pool when decomposers are absent, out of reach, or non-viable, dragging the *available* pool below the floor over time. So nutrient lockup is a **dynamics** failure — turnover- and reach-dependent — not decidable from `N_total` alone, and it sits with the dynamics modes rather than this existence gate. It nonetheless yields to affirmative analysis as far as the committed fluxes allow: a conditional decomposer-return floor and the flux-balance characterisation that follow this gate.

## Resolved finding — nutrient lockup has no clean existence gate (decomposer-return floor)

Nutrient lockup is the dead-pool sibling of energy death ([expected properties](expected-properties.md#nutrient-lockup)): conserved nutrient silts into carcasses faster than living decomposers return it, dragging the *available* pool below the reproduction floor even when `N_total` clears the static energy-death gate above. The tempting move is a closed-form existence gate paralleling extinction's `F ≤ B` — a **decomposer-return floor**: if no decomposer strategy can net-clear metabolism on carcass return, the dead pool never drains and lockup is guaranteed for every seed. On analysis that gate exists only in a degenerate corner and is otherwise blocked by under-commitment — the same shape as the generalist-dominance finding below.

Decomposition is consumption of a carcass (CONTEXT.md, *Decomposition*), so it runs through the committed trophic transfer: a decomposer retains at most `base_trophic_efficiency · exp(−trophic_distance_decay · d)` ([world rules](world-rules.md), flow 7) of a carcass's content, `d` the trait-space distance from the decomposer to the carcass's preserved trait vector. Against this it pays metabolism every tick — `base_metabolic_rate` plus superlinear trait maintenance of its own body (flow 8). So lockup is *avoided* only if some decomposer strategy clears

```
base_trophic_efficiency · (reachable carcass energy per tick)  ≥  base_metabolic_rate + maintenance(decomposer body)
              └──── best-case carcass return ────┘                    └──── decomposition cost ────┘
```

**Why this is not a clean gate — both sides are endogenous.** A carcass holds exactly the structure-bound energy and nutrient of the agent that died; its richness is set by the world's realized energy economy, not by any committed parameter. The decomposer's cost scales with the body it grows, also endogenous. And return *scales with carcass richness* while cost is roughly fixed per tick, so for a rich enough, in-reach carcass decomposition is eventually net-positive — there is no pure-parameter ceiling that universally forbids it. The only **genuinely a priori, universal kills** are the degenerate corners: `base_trophic_efficiency = 0` (no carcass content is ever recoverable) or decomposer reach driven to zero (no carcass is ever contacted). In either, the per-unit-carcass clearance rate is identically zero, the dead pool is a one-way sink, and lockup is certain for every seed. These are real but rarely bite.

**Reserve remedy.** Between the degenerate kills and a healthy cycle, decomposer viability is *turnover- and reach-dependent* — a dynamics property, not a static inequality. A clean closed-form lockup gate becomes available only if the design later commits a bound on **achievable carcass richness** or **decomposer reach** — the two endogenous terms above — a knob it does not currently spend. The design holds this in reserve, to be promoted only if genesis search finds worlds locking up where the committed parameters say a decomposer should be viable — the same contingency posture the generalist-dominance finding below takes toward a cross-trait interaction term. Until then the prevention is real but dynamic, and the matching gate stays out of reach.

## Characterisation — nutrient lockup as a flux balance

Where the finding above shows lockup has no cheap *gate*, the conserved nutrient cycle does admit an affirmative *characterisation* — a closed-form stationary condition derived entirely from committed fluxes. It is **not** a search-box gate: it cannot rule out a region before a run, because it depends on emergent decomposer mass (see the authority note below). Its value is to sharpen the energy-death caveat from a hand-wave into a stated condition, and to give the [validation triad](#place-in-the-validation-triad) its first falsifiable lockup prediction.

Conserved nutrient partitions into three compartments — the **available pool** `A` (the substrate grid), **living-bound** `L` (free store + reproductive earmark + structure-bound), and **carcass-locked** `C` (the dead pool) — with `A + L + C = N_total`, the closed nutrient ledger balanced every tick. The committed phases move nutrient between them: uptake `A → L`, death `L → C` carrying the agent's exact nutrient, and decomposition `C → A` (the trophic drain run over carcass targets). At stationarity the dead-pool balance `dC/dt = 0` fixes the carcass-locked fraction in closed form:

```
C* = death_flux / decomp_turnover
```

— precisely the *"death flux ÷ decomposition rate"* the energy-death gate names but sets aside — where `decomp_turnover` is the per-unit-carcass clearance rate = (decomposer mass) · (the committed `base_trophic_efficiency · exp(−trophic_distance_decay · d)` kernel at the decomposer-on-carcass distance) · (in-reach geometry). The lockup condition is then **the energy-death floor with `N_total` replaced by the *available* budget**: lockup occurs when stranded `C*` pulls the residual pool `A* = N_total − L_min − C*` below the reproduction floor of the energy-death gate. This is the dynamical generalisation that gate says it "cannot yet reach," and it is reachable precisely because every flux above is already a committed rule.

**Authority boundary.** `C*` contains `decomposer mass`, which is *emergent and per-seed*: a decomposer is a behavioural role read from θ, confirmed across seed ensembles but sporadic per-seed, never guaranteed on a single run ([expected properties](expected-properties.md#nutrient-lockup); CONTEXT.md, *Decomposition*). So the characterisation states whether the *average* world strands its nutrient; it can never say *which seeds* nucleate a decomposer guild. That is the same existence-vs-distributional boundary the gates already respect — and the reason `C*` is a characterisation, not a gate.

**Cross-check.** On `scenarios/example9_detrital_pathway.json` the producer ring's carcass rain falls outside any decomposer's reach, so `decomp_turnover → 0` for the field and `C* →` the full death flux: the characterisation predicts **`nutrient_lockup`**, which is the observed verdict the evaluator reports (8/8). This is the cheapest place to hold the affirmative theory falsifiable — agreement raises confidence; a healthy run against an `A`-starving prediction would localise the decomposition-reach geometry as the faulty term.

## Resolved finding — partial closure is the faithful equilibrium, and the committed reach form is not a gate

A standing dead pool is not a defect to be driven to zero — it is the faithful equilibrium of a conserved nutrient stock. Because nutrient is conserved and death is continuous, the carcass-locked compartment `C` is never empty at any non-degenerate fixed point: every tick death refills it and decomposition drains it, and `C* = death_flux / decomp_turnover` is the balance the two strike. `C* → 0` would require either no death or infinite turnover; neither is reachable, and a real ecosystem likewise always carries standing dead organic matter in transit ([nutrient cycling](../ecology/nutrient-cycling.md) — soil organic matter outweighs living biomass and the atmosphere combined). The single-nutrient model has no biologically-produced *refractory* pool that could strand nutrient permanently — substrate-quality recalcitrance (lignin, C:N immobilisation) is a multi-currency phenomenon the model deliberately omits — and the only permanently-inert pool, `unavailable`, is geological and constant. So the question the conserved cycle poses is not "is `C` small?" but **"does the flux balance leave `A*` above the reproduction floor, with `C` bounded?"** — the energy-death floor read on the *available* budget, exactly as the characterisation above states.

`C` is bounded precisely when `decomp_turnover > 0` across the carcass-fall. Where a decomposer persistently contacts the dying, the reached pool cycles at a finite `C*`; where carcass-fall lands outside every decomposer's reach — the un-reached arcs of a ring blanketed by too few point-decomposers — `decomp_turnover → 0` locally and `C` accumulates there. This *geometric* reach limit, not chemical recalcitrance, is the only residual dead pool the model can offer, and whether it bounds `C` overall on a long horizon is a stability question this lens cannot settle a priori (it turns on emergent guild placement and growth) — genesis search, not a knob, is its instrument.

This is why committing nothing leaves the gate status unchanged. The body-extent reach term — `consumption_reach = effective_heterotrophy · (contact_range_coefficient + body_reach_coefficient · √structure)`, a committed world rule ([world rules](world-rules.md), flow 3) — pins the *shape* of reach but not a *bound on its magnitude*: the coefficient is searched and `structure` is emergent, so achievable reach is per-seed and emergent just as decomposer mass is. `decomp_turnover` therefore keeps its emergent factors, the authority boundary holds, and `C*` stays a **characterisation, not a gate**. The clean lockup gate the decomposer-return floor reaches for still requires a *bound* on achievable reach or carcass richness — the knob the design does not spend (above).

The tempting shortcut — pin `body_reach_coefficient > 0` as a world default because a growing mycelium would then blanket the ring and shrink `C` — is rejected on the discipline that keeps every reserve lever unspent: it would choose a parameter to flatter a scenario, not because the ecology fixes its value. The faithful stance is that the reach *form* is committed, its *magnitude* is for genesis to map, and the living/dead physics asymmetry that would widen the detrital niche stays in reserve ([trait space](trait-space.md)) until genesis finds lockup where a decomposer should be viable — a contingency example13 does not trip.

**Empirical confirmation — the loop closes in the conserved-stock sense (#390, extends #136).** `scenarios/example13_closed_web.json` seeds a lean sessile facultative-detritivore guild on `example12`'s viable differentiated ring, within reach of its carcass rain. Across 8 seeds the guild persists as a reproducing lineage (`failure=none` 8/8, fitness 0.686 ≥ example12's 0.667, `trophic_balance_score` 1.0 — a producer-led pyramid, not inverted), and the brown loop carries flux: carcass-locked nutrient sits ~40% below the no-decomposer baseline while `A*` stays above the reproduction floor. By the conserved-stock criterion this **is** closure — the standing `C*` is the faithful equilibrium, not a shortfall. Its residual gap is the geometric one (`body_reach_coefficient = 0`, so the point-decomposers cannot extend reach to the un-reached arcs): the dormant magnitude of a committed form, left for search to explore, not a missing mechanism.

## Resolved finding — generalist dominance has no static gate (by design)

[Expected properties](expected-properties.md) once treated generalist dominance as prevented by superlinear *per-trait* maintenance ("a mathematical property of convex maintenance costs"). On analysis this does not hold: with *independent additive* convex costs, a generalist exploiting two niches at `(t, t)` pays `2·c·t^p` and earns roughly `2·R(t)` — exactly twice a specialist's *net* — and against a specialist at `2t` it even pays *less* (`2·c·t^p < 2^p·c·t^p` for `p > 1`). Per-trait convexity penalises concentration, not breadth; its real role is to make each trait self-limiting. (The implementation does apply this convex per-trait cost — `maintenance_cost_exponent`, default 2 — so the *form* is real and committed; it was the *attribution*, not the cost, that was wrong.)

The system design has since been corrected ([world rules](world-rules.md), cost structure; [expected properties](expected-properties.md), generalist dominance; [trait space](trait-space.md), "No L1 budget constraint"). The anti-generalist force is the conjunction of **structural fragility** (cost-structure trade-off #9 — a broad, high-entropy body crosses its peak-relative death threshold after a smaller structural loss, raising both predation and starvation mortality) and **functional incompatibility** (trade-off #2 — the substrate-anchored stillness autotrophy and nutrient uptake require is broken by mobility, so the broadest generalist cannot physically express its traits). Both are committed mechanisms; neither is a static income/cost inequality. Fragility acts through mortality and incompatibility through morphology — so generalist-dominance prevention is a **dynamics** property (stability of the differentiated state), not an existence gate decidable in closed form. By design there is no clean static gate; this failure mode sits with monoculture and frozen dynamics, beyond cheap a priori reach for now.

A static gate *would* become available if the design committed a **cross-trait interaction maintenance term** (a cost in the product of co-invested traits): a generalist would then be dominated whenever its interaction overhead plus its fragility-driven mortality exceeded its multi-niche income gain — a closed-form inequality. The design holds this term in reserve rather than committing it (it adds a knob and is unnecessary if fragility and incompatibility suffice), to be promoted only if genesis search finds generalists dominating where they should be confined — the same contingency the world rules already name for autotrophy–mobility. Until then the prevention is real but dynamic, and the matching gate stays out of reach.

This is recorded as the first product of the lens: an a priori check that caught a place where the stated mechanism did not produce the expected property, and drove a system-design correction rather than a viability one.

**Empirical confirmation — the prediction holds (#325).** The above was, until tested, a *prediction*: that fragility + incompatibility confine generalists without the interaction term. The example lens now confirms it. `scenarios/example12_generalist_dominance.json` seeds four archetypes in one viable, differentiated world (`clustering_strength` 1.0, `trophic_balance_score` 1.0, survives 2000 ticks on all 8 seeds): specialist producers, specialist mobile consumers, **broad** generalists (autotrophy + heterotrophy + mobility), and **compatible** sessile mixotrophs (autotrophy + heterotrophy) — the broad and compatible pair seeded identically but for mobility (0.5 vs 0.0), an in-run control on the sessile/mobile incompatibility axis. Reading the energy-weighted breadth measure off survivor traits (`probe_generalist`), with `mobility_maintenance_cost = 0`, `wear_rate = 0`, and **no cross-trait interaction term committed**:

- **broad generalists are eliminated** — 0 survivors, 0.0% energy share on every seed, despite an energy advantage and co-location with both light and prey. This is the confined class: the mobile mixotroph (the rooted-producer + roving-hunter the autotrophy–mobility incompatibility rules out);
- **compatible (sessile) mixotrophs persist and now rise to rough parity** — 33–56% of energy (median 44%), at or just under the 50% line and tipping past it on one seed (seed 4: 56%). They are a legal, *non*-incompatible combination (autotrophy + heterotrophy, no mobility), so the design never predicted them confined; fragility alone holds them off runaway, and it does — they co-exist with specialists rather than displacing them;
- specialists hold the majority on most seeds (and the specialist *mobile* consumers, like the broad generalists, are out-competed to 0 here — a competitive loss in this economy, not a feeding failure: see the un-confounding note below); the evaluator's own `generalist_dominance` gate reads 0/8.

So the committed structural fragility (#9) and functional incompatibility (#2) suffice on their own: the differentiated state is stable and the broadest (incompatible) generalist cannot establish. **The interaction term stays in reserve** — the contingency that would promote it (genesis search finding generalists dominating *where they should be confined*) has not fired: the confined class is empty, and the compatible mixotroph that rose to parity is not in that class. The one thing to watch is that the compatible mixotroph's energy share roughly doubled once mobile feeding worked (median 20% → 44%); it sits near parity, not runaway, but if a future sweep shows it crossing decisively into dominance, fragility-alone would be the term under pressure. See [`scenarios/verdicts.md`](../../scenarios/verdicts.md) for the per-seed read.

**Re-validated — the confound is resolved; the prediction holds un-confounded (#380, fixes #379).** The numbers above are from runs **after** the fix for #379. The earlier confirmation had been read from runs in which a contact-duration ramp on consumption (since removed in #380; see [world rules](world-rules.md#consumption-is-incremental-and-its-character-emerges-from-target-and-size)) reset on every movement and so left *any* mobile consumer unable to feed at all — confounding `example12`'s in-run control, since the broad generalists and specialist mobile consumers are mobile (0.5 and 0.3) while the surviving compatible mixotrophs are sessile (0.0). With consumption now a binary-reach drain, mobile consumers demonstrably feed: a dedicated integration test asserts a moving consumer co-located with prey draws real predation energy and survives past tick 2. (The predator–prey `example10_predator_prey_hopf` is *not* such evidence — a per-tick trace (#400) shows its low-mobility consumers fail to establish on the sparse, trait-distant standing crop and go extinct by tick 2, leaving a producer monoculture; its earlier-claimed "signature Hopf oscillation, median 0.36" was finite-N producer-reproduction pulsing, not a trophic cycle, and the mean-field Hopf prediction is recorded as falsified in [`F-hopf-validation.md`](../research/F-hopf-validation.md).) The broad generalist is therefore eliminated *despite being able to feed* — its confinement is attributable to incompatibility (#2) plus fragility (#9), not to broken feeding. The #325 prediction (fragility + incompatibility confine generalists without an interaction term) now has clean, un-confounded support.

## Place in the validation triad

Viability is one of three complementary lenses on the quality of the system design (see [expected properties](expected-properties.md#how-we-test-for-these-properties)):

1. **A priori viability** (this document) — nearly free; rules regions out in closed form.
2. **Diagnostic examples** (`scenarios/`) — a hand-built initial condition probing a specific failure mode, run headless to observe whether it occurs or the system recovers.
3. **[Genesis search](genesis-search.md)** — the broad, expensive empirical sweep of parameter space. It does not return one tuned config; it **illuminates** the space into an *atlas* — a map onto the failure-mode coordinates with a dead frontier of where worlds fail and on which cliff. The atlas's dead frontier is the empirical realisation of this document's negative gates.

This document's gates are not merely *checked* by lens 3 — they are **wired into** it. The two committed closed-form gates (extinction's `F ≤ B`; energy death's `N_total` floor) run as a **prefilter** ahead of the genesis rollouts: a provably-dead config is routed to the atlas's dead frontier without spending an ensemble, shrinking the search box exactly as this document's [payoff](#what-this-document-is-and-is-not) promises. Because the prefilter and the rollout verdict the *same* config, every genesis sweep continuously falsifies the gates — a config gated extinct that survives a rollout localises a mis-drawn gate. The lockup cliff is deliberately *not* prefiltered: it has no cheap gate (it is decomposer-mass-dependent and emergent per seed), so the atlas maps it by running, not gating.

The three are bound together by the **example file**, the only shared concrete object: the *same* initial condition can be predicted by viability, observed by a simulation run, and located relative to the search. That makes the example the unit of cross-validation — a viability gate is a *falsifiable prediction*, and the cheapest place to test it is a single example run. Agreement across the lenses raises confidence; disagreement is diagnostic:

- viability says dead, the run shows life → the gate is wrong or too conservative, or models a mechanism the design lacks;
- viability says nothing, the run fails → the gate set is incomplete, or the failure is a dynamics failure beyond cheap reach.

For this loop to work, an example must carry not just its initial condition but the **failure mode it probes, viability's prediction, and the observed outcome**. Today examples carry only the initial condition; closing that gap is tracked as follow-up work.

## Findings and follow-ups

- **Promote committed forms into system design — done for trophic transfer.** The efficiency-vs-distance form (`base_trophic_efficiency · exp(−trophic_distance_decay · d)`) is now owned by [world rules](world-rules.md) (flow 7), not just parameterised in the scenario files. With the form pinned, the trophic-pyramid-height question moves from conditional to parametric: pyramid height is bounded by how many transfers can each retain enough of the level below to clear metabolism, and the per-transfer ceiling is now a closed form in `base_trophic_efficiency` and `trophic_distance_decay` rather than a free shape.
- **Nutrient lockup — reached as far as the committed fluxes allow.** The carcass-locked nutrient the energy-death gate sets aside now has a closed-form stationary fraction `C* = death_flux / decomp_turnover` (the flux-balance characterisation) and a conditional decomposer-return floor whose clean gate is blocked only by the under-committed carcass-richness/reach terms (the resolved finding). The characterisation makes `example9_detrital_pathway`'s `nutrient_lockup` verdict a falsifiable prediction — viability's first concrete prediction on an existing example, the cross-validation unit the triad turns on. A standing `C*` is itself the faithful equilibrium of the conserved stock, not a deficiency (the partial-closure finding): the criterion is `A*` above the floor with `C` bounded, and the committed body-extent reach form pins reach's shape but not its magnitude, so it does not promote `C*` to a gate (`example13_closed_web` confirms; long-horizon boundedness is genesis-search territory).
- **Anti-generalist mechanism — stated, gate deferred by design.** Prevention now rests on structural fragility (#9) and functional incompatibility (#2); a closed-form generalist-dominance gate becomes possible only if a cross-trait interaction maintenance term is later committed (see the resolved finding above). Tracked as a contingency, not current work.
- **Unify the canonical parameter/form model** across genesis, scenarios, and viability so the three lenses describe the same physics.
